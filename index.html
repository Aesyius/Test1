You got pwned by Helen and Ashwini

No one is immune to social engineering. At its core, social engineering exploits human psychology—our natural trust, curiosity, and willingness to help. Whether it's an email, a phone call, or even a face-to-face interaction, attackers know how to manipulate our instincts and emotions to gain access to sensitive information. It's not about being "naive" or "weak"; it's about the way our minds are wired to respond to social cues. We all need to stay vigilant and aware because, in the end, anyone can fall victim to a well-crafted story or a seemingly innocent request. Being cautious and skeptical is not a sign of distrust—it's a sign of awareness and self-protection.
